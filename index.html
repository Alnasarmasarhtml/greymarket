01 — THE EDGE理

The crowd
is the price.

Everyone sells into the same six ports. The more a port is used, the less it pays. So the money is in the port nobody's using yet.

訳 Pumping out more goods and dumping them all in one busy port earns LESS than a small, smart sale into an empty one. The money is in the empty space — not the volume.

The twist: you don't sell now. You commit a sealed order that clears one batch later — and the crowd moves first. So the skill isn't spotting which port is quiet. It's calling which one stays quiet.

解 — THE DECODER対訳

The ink says /
it means.

A translator's key. Every mark on the floor, in plain words. Read once — you're inside.

乾DRY
An empty port. Almost no one selling here, so it pays the most. Sell here.
混OPEN
A middling port. Some crowd, okay pay. Fine, not great.
密SOAKED
A crowded port. Everyone dumped here — pays almost nothing. Avoid.
兆INCOMING
Empty now, but the crowd is about to flood it next batch. Looks good — won't be. (Shows only with INTEL.)
INK · the blot
How crowded a port is, shown as ink. Bigger, darker blot = more goods dumped = lower pay.
CROWD % (THRU)
How crowded a port is, 0–100%. 0 = empty, 100 = jammed. Higher crowd = lower pay. (DeFi calls this congestion / price-impact.)
↑ YIELD · pay-rate
How much each unit pays right now, 0–100. ↑ high = good, ↓ low = dead. The flip side of crowding.
THE MANIFOLD 需給曲線
The instrument under the grid. Tall bars = crowded ports. The line that droops into a valley is the pay-rate — it sags over the crowd. The one bright tick is your pick. Sell where the line rides high.
COMMIT SELL 封印
Place a sealed sell order. You don't get the price you see now — it locks and clears at the next batch, after the crowd moves.
SEALED · RESOLVING
Your order is locked and you can't watch it. Everyone seals blind, then all clear together at the batch — no peeking, no speed-racing. (DeFi calls this "commit-reveal": you commit hidden, the batch reveals.)
BATCH
The clock — every ~12s all sealed orders clear at once. The crowd moves first, then your sale clears. Faster with FENCE SPEED.
GHOST PRE-STAMP 兆
The faint ring around a port = "the crowd is about to pile in here." The game's forecast. Only shows with INTEL.
INTEL 諜
An upgrade bought with $GREY. Reveals the forecast — which empty port is about to flood, and higher levels show how hard. This is the edge.
STANDING ORDER
Autopilot. Auto-sells into the quietest port each batch, even offline. No foresight — so playing yourself beats it. The crowd will find it.
CASH ¥
Your main money, from sales. Buys haulers, bigger holds, faster batches, quieter selling.
$GREY
The scarce token, a trickle per sale (~1 per ¥3,000 sold). Spends only on INTEL. Save ~1 $GREY (about ¥3,000 of sales) to buy your first INTEL — that's what it's for. (prototype · off-chain · not money)
YOUR STAIN 汚 · PRICE IMPACT
Your own selling crowds the port too. Sell big into one place and you crash your own price — for the next ton, and future-you. DISCRETION softens it. Selling is never free.
DEMAND WINDOW
Each port's baseline pay drifts slowly on a hidden cycle. Learn the rhythm — the deepest, discoverable edge.
ANTI-CORRELATED
Fancy word for "go where the crowd isn't." The best move is the opposite of what everyone's doing — and it stays that way, because the instant everyone floods the quiet port it stops being quiet.
問 — STRAIGHT ANSWERS問答

No riddles here.

What is this?
An idle game about smuggling. Trucks make goods on their own; you sell them at whichever of six ports is least crowded. Selling crashes the price, so the skill is selling where the crowd isn't. The poetry is the skin. The game underneath is simple.
How do I make money?
Pick a 乾 DRY (empty) port, set a sell volume, hit COMMIT SELL. It clears at the next batch and pays CASH (¥) — empty ports pay the most. Reinvest the cash in more trucks and bigger holds; the in-game economy snowballs from there.
Why did my sale pay so little?
The port was crowded — or the crowd flooded it between sealing and clearing. You also dent the price yourself when you dump a lot at once. Smaller sells into truly empty ports keep more.
What's a "dry" port?
An empty, lightly-used one (low CROWD %). Fewer sellers means each unit pays more. 乾 = dry = the negative space that pays — where nobody's selling.
What does INTEL do?
It shows the forecast — a ghost ring (兆 INCOMING) on the empty port that's about to get crowded, so you don't sell into a trap. Higher levels show how hard, and how soon.
How do I get INTEL?
INTEL costs $GREY, the trickle-token from every sale (~1 $GREY per ¥3,000 sold). Sell a few times, save up about 1 $GREY, and buy INTEL NET in THE OPERATION panel.
Does it earn while I'm offline?
Goods keep stacking offline, always. Cash only if STANDING ORDER is on — but it sells without foresight, so active play earns more.
Why are my orders sealed? I can't even watch.
By design. Everyone commits blind and the batch clears at once — no speed games, just your read of where the crowd's going.
Is $GREY real money? Is this financial advice?
No and no. Prototype · off-chain simulation. $GREY, ports and yields are illustrative game state — not financial anything.
